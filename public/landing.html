We know what you're thinking

Your objections are valid.
Let's address them honestly.

But we already use WhatsApp.
That's the point. WhatsBoard does not replace WhatsApp — it organizes what happens after the chat starts. You still sell on WhatsApp. You just stop losing orders in it. WhatsApp for selling. WhatsBoard for controlling.
My team is small. Just me and one other person.
That's exactly who WhatsBoard is built for. One person running a business from a phone benefits more from order clarity than a team of twenty. Stop keeping orders in your head. Your brain has better things to do.
We are not tech people. This looks complicated.
Setup takes 5 minutes. If you can add a contact on WhatsApp, you can use WhatsBoard. There is no training needed. The whole design is built around how East African sellers already think and work.
Can't we just continue using chats and notes?
You can. But you already know you're losing orders. You already know nimetuma creates confusion. You already know "nitachukua kesho" becomes nothing. The cost of continuing is real money. Not a hypothetical.
What if my customers don't want to change how they order?
Your customers keep ordering on WhatsApp, Instagram, TikTok — exactly as before. Nothing changes for them. You are the only one who gets a better system behind the scenes. They just notice you are now more organized and faster to respond.
Is this only for big businesses?
No. WhatsBoard was specifically designed for small and medium sellers in East Africa — fashion sellers, beauty stores, food sellers, gadget traders, and anyone else who gets customers on social media and chat. The more "small" you are, the more you benefit.
