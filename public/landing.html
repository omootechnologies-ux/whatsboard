The Diagnosis

You didn't lose the customer.
You lost control of the chat. 😮

Every online seller in Tanzania knows this feeling. Business is "going," but money isn't adding up. Here's exactly where it's leaking.

Pain 01

The Screenshot Archive Problem

Your order history is 600 screenshots in your gallery. Looking for a customer's delivery address takes 15 minutes and a prayer.

"Nitatuma picha ya receipt..." — and now it's buried under 400 memes.
Pain 02

"Nimetuma" Sent. Payment: ??

They said they sent money. Maybe they did. Maybe they didn't. You feel awkward asking again. So you pack the order and... hope.

"Nimetuma!" — 3 hours ago. M-Pesa notification: still nothing.
Pain 03

Memory-Based Operations

You are the database. Your brain holds 40 pending orders, 12 unpaid customers, and 7 "nitachukua kesho" promises. One WhatsApp notification wipes it all.

"Nilikuambia wiki iliyopita... hujakumbuka?" 😅
Pain 04

Dispatch Became a Phone Call

"Ume-dispatch?" turns into 3 calls, 2 chats, and someone walking to the packing room. In 2025. For a business with real customers.

"Niko karibu Mbezi, order yangu iko wapi?" — nightmare.
Pain 05

The "Nitachukua Kesho" Graveyard

They said tomorrow. Tomorrow came. Nobody followed up. The sale died quietly while you were managing 30 other chats.

Repeat customers exist. You just don't know who they are.
Pain 06

5 Apps, Zero Visibility

Orders from WhatsApp, TikTok comments, Instagram DMs, Facebook inbox, Telegram. Your "dashboard" is five apps open at once and a notepad.
